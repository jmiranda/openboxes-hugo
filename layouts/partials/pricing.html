FAQ

Is OpenBoxes really "free"?

Yes, OpenBoxes is free to download and use. We do not charge you for user licenses and other fees. In other words, you are free to install the software on your laptop or a server that you manage without having to pay us for user licenses and other fees.

If OpenBoxes is "free" then why do I need to pay for it?

OpenBoxes is free to download and use.

But unless you plan to install OpenBoxes on your personal laptop or desktop, you'll have costs associated with operating the software. Read our Cost Analysis Guide to better understand the costs associated with adopting and using OpenBoxes.

Why is Professional Support more expensive than hosting?

Supporting software is time-consuming and expensive. In order to provide support to our users we need a team to cover our support hours even on days when there are no support requests.

If our support packages are too expensive, we highly recommend that you use our Community self-service support options until you need a guaranteed level of support.

We would also like to encourage third-party resellers to contact us if you'd like to offer support and hosting. We don't need a partnership agreement. We just need to ensure that you plan to take care of our users as well as we would.

Can I become a partner so that I can provide hosting and support to OpenBoxes users?

Yes, please. Yes. We don't have an official partnership agreement, but we'd love to promote companies that are willing to provide services to our users. Contribute to this discussion on our forum if you're interested in providing services.

    Does OpenBoxes provide 24/7 support?

    At the moment, we provide support for US Business Hours. However, we can provide 24/7 support to accommodate business outside of the US. Please email support@openboxes.com if you would like to discuss 24/7 support options.

    How do I add my own features?

    There are basically two ways to customize the software:

    1. Hire us and we'll take care of all the complexity of gathering requirements, developing, testing, and release your features.
    2. Hire your own developers and contribute the code back to core (or to your own fork).1

    If you are interested in option (1) please contact us at (support@openboxes.com) to discuss your requirements and we'll provide a proposal with a quote and timeline for your feature request.

    1 Please take caution with maintaining your own fork because you could very well end up in a situation where you lose the ability to pull the latest code from core into your fork. Don't hesitate to contact us for recommendations if you do go down this route.

    Do people actually ask these questions ... frequently?

    No. Literally no one has ever asked a frequently asked question. Ever. Everything you have ever been told is a lie.
